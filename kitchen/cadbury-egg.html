Reimagining the Cadbury Egg

The Cocktail That Fixed Easter Candy

My eyes fly open and I leap out of bed. It’s Easter!
Oh. Um. Right. First:
“Dear God, thanks for Jesus. That’s pretty incredible… you’re awesome.”

Ok, now I only have an hour before we leave for church. Sixty minutes to find the glorious treasure trove of chocolate, eat as much as I can stomach, and still get my fancy new Easter dress on before we leave.

I look around my room. There. On the dresser: my first clue.

After you wake and before you eat,
This is the place that keeps you neat.

Easy enough. We all know as a 13 year old girl I’m spending way too much time in front of the bathroom mirror in the mornings! There I find another clue.

This clue is on some shelves
So go and take a look
It's hidden among words and pictures
Inside your favorite…

Book!

I rush over to The Bread Sister of Sinking Creek—a signed version, thank you very much—to find the next clue.

What has many rings but no fingers?

Oh. This is gonna slow me down...

What gets warm and turns around,
but never leaves its place?

The dryer.

I hurry to the basement and throw the door open. There it is.

It’s a giant pink plastic basket, and it’s all for me.

It’s beautiful. Nestled in the neon green Easter grass are Skittles, M&Ms, Butterfingers, Pixie Stix, Fun Dip, and a couple Ring Pops.

And there, in the middle, sits my giant peanut butter egg. Milk chocolate. The size of my head. Filled with sweet, salty, creamy peanut butter.

As I pass the kitchen table, I see that my brother has found his basket. It’s filled to the brim with Cadbury eggs.

I love the way the basket looks with all those bright blue, foil covered eggs, but I shudder thinking about biting into one.

We head to church, slightly sick from sugar, and the back row softly crinkled wrappers all through service.

Looking back on it now, I can see the preparation that went into those mornings, the work that made it seem effortless.

My Easters were perfect. A little chaos, far too much sugar, and overflowing with people who loved me deeply.

My basket always had what I actually wanted.

And still, every year, I found myself looking at those Cadbury eggs.

I didn’t like the candy, but I always wished I could be part of it.

Some things never change.

I’m still the only one who notices spring by crocuses and birdsong instead of grocery store displays.

I don’t need to be part of the Cadbury Cream Egg Club.

My mom encouraged me to be exactly who I was.

That mattered.

Being noticed, known, and accepted meant I didn’t have to change.

That kind of freedom is hard to unlearn.

I never had to conform, and that let me experiment. To adjust. To trust my instincts.

This year, I decided to change things.

I may not want that syrupy center—but I could transform the idea.

So I experimented.

Melted Cadbury eggs? Nope. Cloying.

Then it hit me.

Egg coffee.

A velvety shot of espresso. Ice-cold vodka. A pinch of salt.

A glossy whipped egg cream.

Fizzy milk. Freshly grated dark chocolate.

It’s nostalgic. Familiar. But finally fits.

I didn’t need to learn to like Cadbury eggs.

I just had to make them my own.

🐣 Cadbury Cream Fizz

Ingredients

  • 1–2 oz carbonated milk
  • 0.5 oz Baileys
  • 1.5 oz espresso
  • 1 oz vodka
  • pinch of salt
  • 1 egg yolk
  • 1 tsp sugar + 1 tsp hot water
  • 1 drop vanilla
  • dark chocolate

Instructions

  1. Mix Baileys into cold carbonated milk
  2. Shake espresso, vodka, salt
  3. Make syrup
  4. Whisk into egg yolk
  5. Layer on top
  6. Finish with chocolate

Note: Carbonate milk using soda maker or seltzer.
